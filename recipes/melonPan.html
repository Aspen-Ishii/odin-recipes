Melon Pan メロンパン

Description

Melonpan is a type of sweet bread that originated in Japan . Nationally, it is a type of bread made by placing sweet biscuit dough (cookie dough) on top of a round dough, and is mostly baked with a lattice pattern on the surface, but there are many different types with different appearances and materials

Ingredients

Bread dough:

  • 150 g bread flour
  • 25 g sugar
  • 30 g Eggs
  • 60cc lukewarm water
  • 15g butter
  • 2g instant dry yeast
  • 2g salt

Biscuit dough:

  • 100 g cake flour
  • 50 granulated sugar
  • 30 g egg
  • 35 g butter

Outer Coating:

  • 25 g sugar

Steps:

Biscuit Dough:

  1. Mix room temperature butter and sugar together to make mayonnaise. Add the beaten eggs in 2 or 3 portions and mix well each time.
  2. Add the sifted flour and mix in a cutting motion to combine.
  3. Wrap the cookie dough in plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator while you make the bread dough.

    4. Bread dough

    1. Place strong flour and dry yeast in a bowl and mix with a whisk.
    2. Mix the eggs, lukewarm water, sugar, and salt well and pour into the bowl containing the strong flour and dry yeast.
    3. Roughly mix with a spatula so that the moisture is distributed throughout the flour.
    4. Turn out the rough dough onto the counter and knead it using the entire palm of your hand, stretching the dough out onto the counter. Knead until the dough stops sticking to your hands and is smooth.
    5. Add room temperature butter and knead some more. When you stretch the dough so thin that you can see your fingers through it, it's done kneading. *The butter will jump out and be sticky at first, but it will come together as you knead.
    6. Roll the dough so that it is flat and place it in a bowl with the closed side facing down. Cover with plastic wrap and let rise at 40℃ for about 30 minutes until the dough has doubled in size. *If you do not have an oven with a fermentation function, let the dough rise slowly in a warm room until it doubles in size.
    7. Divide the dough after primary fermentation into 6 equal parts, roll each into a smooth ball, cover with plastic wrap, and let rest at room temperature for 15 minutes.

    Forming and Baking

    1. Press the rested dough firmly with your palm to release the gas, then roll it again. Pinch the closed eye firmly with your fingertips to close it.
    2. Divide the cookie dough that has been resting in the refrigerator into 6 equal parts, roll them into balls, wrap them in plastic wrap, and roll them out with a rolling pin. Roll it out so that it is one size larger than the bread dough.
    3. Cover the rolled bread dough with cookie dough and gently shape it with your hands. It's best if you can see the 500 yen coin-sized dough on the back side.
    4. P ut granulated sugar (1 to 2 tablespoons) for topping in a bowl and roll the surface of the melon bread dough to coat it with granulated sugar. Once the granulated sugar is firmly attached, use a card or knife to make four incisions in each direction. *If the cut is shallow, the mesh will be slightly meshed, and if it is deep, the cookie dough will crack. Adjust the depth of the cut according to your preference.
    5. Arrange the melon bread dough on a cookie sheet and let it ferment for about 50 minutes at 35℃. When the dough becomes larger, it is a sign that fermentation is complete. The key is to ferment it in a dry state without wrapping it. *Again, if you don't have an oven with a fermentation function, let the dough rise indoors until it becomes a little larger. Also, please do not wrap it.
    6. Preheat the oven to 190℃, add the fermented melon bread dough and bake for about 12 minutes. It's done when the surface is golden brown.
